BMI WEIGHT RANGES

Adults

In the range of normal weight, a BMI calculation falls between 18.5 and 24.9. The normal weight level is 18.5 to 23 for minority ethnic groups like Blacks, Asians, and some others. For those of White ancestry, a BMI of 18.5 or less indicates underweight, 18.5 to 24.9 indicates healthy weight, 25 to 29.9 indicates overweight, and 30 or more indicates obesity. A lower BMI is associated with a higher risk of long-term illnesses such type 2 diabetes among Black, Asian, and several other minority ethnic groups. With a BMI of 23 or above, individuals from these groups are at elevated risk of being overweight; with 27.5 or higher, they are at high risk of being obese.

Children's BMI

The BMI calculator accounts for age, gender, and height and weight for kids and teenagers between the ages of 2 and 18. The BMI of a child is expressed as a "centile". The centile value provides a percentage comparison of their BMI to that of other kids their age and sex. A girl at the seventy-fifth centile, for a case, kg that is greater than seventy five out of each and every hundred females her age. The BMI calculator determines if a kid or young person is: underweight if they are on the second centile or lower; a healthy weight if they are between the second and 91st centiles; overweight if they are on the 91st centile or above; or severely overweight if they are on the 98th centile or above.

Waist to height ratio

Despite the fact that you have a good BMI, evaluating the waist to your height can reveal whether you have too much belly fat. Calculate the circumference of your waist and divide this number by your height in order to find your waist-to-height ratio. Use measurements that are expressed in the same units, such as centimeters or inches.

A waist-to-height ratio of 0.50 or greater indicates you may be at higher risk for conditions like type 2 diabetes, heart disease, and stroke. Your waist size is:

  1. Locate the highest point of your hips (merely above the belly button) and the bottom of your ribcage.
  2. Measure your waist halfway between these points using a tape measure.
  3. Before measuring the measurement, let your breath out freely.

Waist circumference

With the measuring instrument parallel to the floor, you should measure the circumference of your waist halfway between the top of your hipbones and the bottom of the rib cage. When you take the measurement, you must be exhaling.

Male

  • Increased health risk: Having greater than or equal to 94cm
  • High health risk: Having greater than or equal to 102cm

Female

  • Increased health risk: Having greater than or equal to 80cm
  • High health risk: Having greater than or equal to 88cm

Waist to hip ratio

Calculate the circumference of your waist (as described above) and hip length at the broadest portion of your hips to get your waistline to hip ratio. To calculate the ratio, divide your hip measurement by your belly circumference.

  • A waistline to hip ratio of more than 0.85 in females is linked to a higher-than-average risk.
  • A waistline to hip ratio of more than 1.00 in men is linked to higher-than-average risk.
